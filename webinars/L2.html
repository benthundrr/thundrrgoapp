03 · The Posting Engine

How to Hit 100 Posts a Month

100 posts looks impossible until you stop filming one video at a time. The creators who hit it film in batches and post from a queue.

🏨
Treat one property as 8–10 posts, not oneLobby, room reveal, the view, the bathroom, amenities, the neighborhood, a "what $X gets you" comparison, a morning routine. Film it all in one visit.
🎥
Batch-film 2–3 days a week, never dailyTwo solid filming days can stock a week of posts. Editing and posting become a 20-minute daily habit, not a production.
🗓️
Keep a 7-day rolling queueAlways have at least a week of edited posts ready. A queue means a slow week or a travel day never breaks your streak.
📈
Math: 3–4 posts/day = 100/monthFrom two filming days a week, that's ~12–16 clips to bank — entirely doable from 1–2 locations.

✦ The Format System

Lock Your Content Recipe

Top earners don't reinvent each post — they run a fixed recipe and only swap the destination. A locked format means all your energy goes to volume and markets, not decisions.

① Hook · 0–2s
Best visual + place & payoff on screen. No walk-ups, no "hey guys."
② Body · 3–5 shots
3–5s clips — room, view, amenity, detail. Fast cuts, no dead air.
③ Length · locked lane
Your proven runtime — ~15–25s — every time. Consistency compounds.
④ Close · the offer
Spoken + on-screen CTA: name the price, point to the link.
🧪
Write your recipe down once. When the format is fixed, a new post is just "same recipe, new hotel" — that's how creators hit 100 a month without burning out.
05 · Get Found

Advanced SEO Strategy

TikTok GO is a search engine first. Buyers type their plans into the search bar — your caption is how they find you weeks after you post.

🎣 Go long-tail
"luxury boutique hotels in [neighborhood]" beats "hotels." Specific phrases have less competition and far higher intent.
🗣️ Speak buyer, not blogger
Use the words a traveler would type — "where to stay near Disney," not "wanderlust escape."
📝 Write denser captions
Keyword-rich captions get surfaced for more searches. Name the city, the property, and the occasion.
📅 Refresh weekly
Check what's trending in TikTok search each week and aim a few posts at the rising terms.

🎯 Go deep on 3–5

✦ The Funnel

Know Your Numbers

Every sale flows through four stages. When something's off, diagnose one stage — don't rebuild the whole post. Each metric points to a specific fix:

👀 Views
Reach. Weak? Stronger hook + better tags.
👆 CTR
Clicks. Weak? Clearer caption + visible CTA.
💰 CVR
Orders. Weak? Name the offer, show the link out loud.
🧾 AOV
Order size. Lift it? Feature higher-value stays.
🔬
Most "my views don't convert" problems are a CTR or CVR issue — not a reach problem. Fix the stage that's actually leaking, and the whole funnel lifts.
09 · Close the Loop

The Weekly Review Loop

📆
Every Sunday, 20 minutes — pull the week's posts and sort by what each one did. Then act on three simple rules:
💰 Drove orders?
Double down. Post more from that market and that format this week.
👆 High CTR, low orders?
Rewrite the CTA. The hook works; the close doesn't. Make the offer explicit.
👀 Views, no clicks?
Add the product earlier. People watched but never knew there was something to buy.
🔁
One change a week compounds. Replicate your top post's formula — same length, same hook style, same tag mix — instead of starting from scratch every time.
