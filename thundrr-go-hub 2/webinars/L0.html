06 · Where to Film

Film Local, Not the Crowds

The biggest cities are the most crowded — dozens of creators film the same hotels. The hidden gems near you have less competition and convert better.

🗺️
Film what's near you — the boutique inn, the local resort, the small-town stay
🚗
You don't need to travel — your best market is probably 30 minutes away
🎯
Fewer creators competing means more eyes on your video
💡
In our data, an overlooked market out-earned all of Los Angeles by 4× — proof that you should film where others aren't.
07 · Skill · Filming

Shooting Checklist

Run this every time you film — all free, all on your phone.

1
Open on your best shotThe first 1–2 seconds decide if people keep watching. Lead with the wow.
2
Shoot vertical (9:16), steady & high-qualityTwo hands or brace on a rail — no blurry or shaky footage.
3
Use natural light — morning or late afternoonFace the windows; golden hour looks best on camera.
4
Capture variety — clips of 5–8 seconds eachWide shots, close-ups, the pool, the view, the room — feeds 8–10 posts.
5
Show the key selling points (USPs)Authentic, on-site footage of what makes the stay special — don't skip the standout features.
08 · Skill · Editing

Editing Checklist

1
Edit smoothly — cut clips to 3–5 secondsKeep it structured; no abrupt or jarring cuts.
2
Keep it concise (30–90 sec)Long enough to sell, short enough to finish. Don't pad with long clips.
3
Add overlays, subtitles or a voiceoverDon't rely only on audio — most people watch on mute. Add trending sound on top.
4
End on the booking cueFinal frame points to the link — close every video with intent.
09 · Captions

Write a Caption That Sells

Every caption follows one simple formula. Don't stop at one line — tell the story.

① Location
Open with the place name — it's your #1 keyword.
② What you're showing
The pool, the view, the room — paint the picture.
③ Call to action
End with "Link in bio" or "Tap to book."
✍️
Posts that sold had captions about 3× longer than the rest. Put the destination in your first two lines and tell the full story.

11 · Consistency

Post Every Day

Consistency beats talent. The creators who earned posted on most days of the month — not in one big burst.

📆
Start with 1–2 posts a day — that pace gets you to your first sale in two weeks
🔁
Batch-film one visit, then post from drafts dailyFilm once, post for days — never post from scratch same-day.
⏰

12 · Official Content Guideline

Know the Basics

Straight from the official TikTok GO ACC playbook — the do's and don'ts that get content approved and earning.

✓ Do
  • Include a clear CTA with booking steps
  • Use overlays, subtitles or voiceovers
  • Highlight key selling points (USPs)

  • Use authentic, on-site footage

✕ Don't
  • Use stock or internet photos
  • Be "salesy" or over-promotional
  • Make it random or chaotic

  • Use long clips or jarring cuts
  • Skip key features or rely only on audio

What to Expect

Week 1 · Start
Set up and just start posting. Views may be low while the account warms up — totally normal.
Week 2 · Rhythm

Week 3 · Compound
Captions and markets dialed in. Reach grows and clicks start coming in.

First sales begin landing here — and often between day 30 and 60.
